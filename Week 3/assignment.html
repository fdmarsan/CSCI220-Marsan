Experiment of the Effects of Caffeine on GPA

Conducted by Jennifer (who works in a lab)

This experiment seeks to determine if a correlation exists between the amount of caffeine an individual consumes daily and that individual's grade point average (GPA) in school.

100 students were assigned numbers from 1-100 and were chosen at random into three groups:

  • Group A: received caffeine equivalent to the national average (3 cups)
  • Group B: received twice the amount of the national daily average (6 cups)
  • Group C: recieved a placebo (control group)

Steps followed to produce results:

  1. 100 students selected from volunteer applicants between the ages of 18-22 years old. Equal numbers of female and male participants with a starting GPA between 3.2 and 3.3
  2. Each student assigned a number from 1-100
  3. Numbers were randomly selected and assigned to groups A, B, and C
  4. Group A was given 3 cups of coffee per day (the national average)
  5. Group B was given 6 cups of coffee per day
  6. Group C was given a placebo
  7. Grade Point Averages were tracked throughout the term and averaged by group at the start of the semester , at midterm , and after final grades were submitted.
